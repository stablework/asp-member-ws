\par {\b How to Interview a Banker/Merchant}\par \par Banker/Merchants are very adept socially with a banker's formality and appropriateness and a Merchant's warmth and charm. They are extraordinary in their ability to learn everything from technical to human development and emotional intelligence. These people pride themselves on their knowledge, their practical approach and their vision of the future.\par \par because their banker energy conserves energy and resources they are uniquely prepared to take on any new opportunities their Merchant values may uncover. They test and prove likelihood of success and position themselves for success well in advance. Yet they are spontaneous in their actions and socially gregarious at times. They are good team builders.\par \par Banker/Merchants may appear and actually erratic at times, being cool and unemotional for long periods, yet suddenly flashing with overt caring and charisma. When a person experiences the Merchant values first in these individuals, they may feel set up and disappointed by a conservative force and a negative response to new ideas, coming from the protective and thoughtful banker values. Watch for a tendency to judge others harshly or to claim a victim position in situations.\par \par Since bankers and Merchants tend to avoid conflict, these people can be passive aggressive, finding ways to politic behind the scenes with solid facts and figures and with a whining charm that draws people in. If banker/Merchants choose to act out they are generally very capable of undermining an organization, stalling forward motion and gaining control through background political tactics.\par \par {\b Here are a few questions you may want to ask. }\par \par A. Tell me about a success that was created by your conservative nature combined with your people skills. How have you built on this success?\par \par b. What area of knowledge and expertise do you consider your most important asset? Can you remember a time when sharing that knowledge or giving away your expertise was not a wise thing to do? How did others respond to your withholding of information? Were you able to resolve the situation successfully?\par \par C. Tell me about a time when you took a chance, put your resources seriously at risk? Were you able to remain constant with your original commitments? \par \par D. Can you remember a time when you held back and didn't pursue something that was exciting and attractive to you? Do you still consider the decision a right decision?\par \par E. Tell me about your experience with a person or situation that you consider the most unfair or difficult in your life. Were you able to set things right in this situation?\par \par F. Tell me about a time you were able to gather people around you to cause a change in your company's direction or specific course of action. What kind of conflict did you create? How was the conflict resolved?\par \par G. Can you remember any instance when you made a projection or promised to work on something that you realized was not right? How quickly did you correct the situation and stop the activity? What leadership skills did you learn from this experience?\par \par {\b Banker/Merchant Lowest Score -- Builder}\par \par H. Have you ever lost a position of leadership or responsibility to someone who was more assertive than you? When was the last time you let someone keep you from getting what you wanted?\par \par I. Tell me about the hardest decision you have ever had to make. Were you concerned with the way others would judge you if you made a poor decision?\par \par J. Have you ever spent time planning and developing a team of people when someone else thought you should just get started and do the work yourself? How do you motivate yourself to get into action and make risky decisions?\par \par K. When was the last time you gave someone a command or a clear requirement for them to take action. How do you respond when someone gives you a command or strong requirement for immediate action?\par \par {\b Banker/Merchant Lowest Score -- Innovator}\par \par L. When was the last time you made a commitment or set a goal you simply were unable or unwilling to keep? Tell me about the time when you were most tenacious-when you continued to struggle even when others thought you should give up.\par \par M. Can you remember a time when your energy and creativity created a situation that required new systems or processes in order to move forward? How did you make this happen?\par \par N. Have you ever found yourself at a loss for good solutions to real problems? Who did you get to help you?\par \par O. Tell me about a time when you knew what you wanted or what you wanted to do, but you had difficulty knowing how to achieve your desired goal. How did you solve your dilemma?\par \par P. Can you remember a situation that you were required to control, preserving resources, but there were inadequate systems in place? How did you decide what systems were required? Who helped you design the processes and procedures?\par \par Q. Is there one a situation in your past that was particularly frustrating? Were there people around you that you could count on to support your objectives? What would your team members from that time remember most positively? Would those people jump at the chance to work with you again? If not, what skills have you learned to make you a better team player.\par \par R. What's the biggest price you ever paid for either not having a good plan, or not following the plan that you created, or was created for you? How have you assured yourself that you will not pay that price again?
